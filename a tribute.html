Corrie Ten Boom

Woman who saved the lives of Jews

  • April 15, 1892: Cornelia "Corrie" ten Boom is born in Haarlem, Netherlands.
  • 1937: The ten Boom family begins using their home as a safe haven for Jewish refugees and members of the Dutch underground.
  • May 1940: The Nazis invade the Netherlands, beginning a period of occupation.
  • February 28, 1944: The ten Boom family is betrayed and arrested by the Gestapo. Corrie, her sister Betsie, and their father Casper are taken into custody.

  • 1950s-1970s: Corrie travels extensively, speaking about her experiences and the power of forgiveness.
  • 1971: Corrie's book, "The Hiding Place," co-authored with John and Elizabeth Sherrill, is published. It becomes an international bestseller and is later made into a movie in 1975.
  • 1977: Corrie moves to Placentia, California, where she continues to write and speak.
  • April 15, 1983: Corrie ten Boom passes away on her 91st birthday in Placentia, California.
  • 2008: Corrie ten Boom is honored by the Yad Vashem Holocaust Memorial in Israel as one of the Righteous Among the Nations for her efforts to save Jews during the Holocaust.
